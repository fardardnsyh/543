Design Process

Planning

When determining what my website was going to look like, I had a different vision in mind. The original plan was to use a green colour scheme but the colour combinations did not align with the profile image. Additionally, the goal was to fulfill the criteria of this assignment but also add a personal touch to the design. This is why I decided that the website may not need to look extra professional, and adding a few fonts or images or animations that matched my interests would not be an issue. Another factor I considered was implementing heuristics and designing thinking elements to ensure that the user experience (UX) is effective. In order to do this, the information architecture (IA) required easier navigation, hence making it easy for people to find information. Part of design thinking is the importance of accessibility, which meant that I needed to make sure that all texts were eligible. More about accessibility will be discussed.

Inspiration

My work was inspired from the more popular inspirational app, Pinterest. I tend to search for website designs and try to select pieces that are suitable for a professional website (...and easy to recreate). The main page for the website and the usage of multiple eclipse elements. Aside from that, normal google searches gave me a lot of ideas regarding what kinds of elements and animations I can add.

WCAG2.0 Accessibility

Perceivable: The website provide an alternative for elements that are not text, for instance, images, videos, etc. All text used are of high contrast with the background, and all fonts are eligible

Operable: Elements and images cannot result in serious health concerns, like flashing lights for seizures

Understandable: Navigation is predictable and consistent for all pages. text content Text is readable and understandable
